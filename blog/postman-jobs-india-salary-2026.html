Postman Jobs in India 2026: Salary, Eligibility & How to Apply

📅 June 2026  ·  ⏱ 6 min read  ·  ✍️ HireTrack Team
📮

Postman jobs remain one of the most stable and sought-after government roles in India. With India Post operating the largest postal network in the world — over 1.5 lakh post offices — there is steady demand for postmen, mail guards and Gramin Dak Sevaks (GDS). This 2026 guide covers what a postman job involves, the latest salary, eligibility, exam pattern and how to apply.

What is a Postman Job?

A postman delivers letters, parcels, money orders and registered post to addresses within an assigned beat. The role exists in two broad streams:

  • Government (India Post): Postman, Mail Guard and Gramin Dak Sevak (GDS) roles under the Department of Posts. Permanent, pensionable, with allowances.
  • Private courier: Delivery executives at companies like Blue Dart, DTDC, Delhivery and e-commerce logistics firms. Higher mobility, performance incentives.

Government postmen also receive Dearness Allowance (DA), House Rent Allowance (HRA), Transport Allowance and a pension — pushing total in-hand value well above the basic pay.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Education: Class 10 (Matriculation) pass from a recognised board. GDS requires Class 10 with Mathematics and English as subjects.
  • Age: 18 to 27 years (relaxations: OBC +3 years, SC/ST +5 years, PwD as per rules).
  • Language: Must have studied the local/regional language up to Class 10.
  • Other: Basic computer knowledge and a two-wheeler driving licence are preferred for delivery beats.

How to Apply for a Postman Job

  1. Visit the official India Post recruitment portal: indiapostgdsonline.gov.in (for GDS) or the relevant SSC / postal circle notification for Postman & Mail Guard posts.
  2. Register and watch for your state/circle-wise notification.
  3. Fill the application form, upload your Class 10 marksheet, photo and signature.
  4. Pay the application fee (where applicable) and submit before the deadline.
  5. For Postman/Mail Guard, prepare for the computer-based test; for GDS, selection is based on Class 10 merit.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the salary of a postman in India in 2026?

A government postman (India Post) earns a basic pay starting at ₹21,700 per month under Pay Level 3, rising to around ₹69,100 with experience and allowances. Private courier delivery agents typically earn ₹15,000 to ₹25,000 per month.

What is the eligibility for a postman job in India Post?

You must have passed Class 10 (Matriculation) from a recognised board, be aged 18 to 27 years (with relaxations for reserved categories), and know the local language. A valid driving licence is often preferred for delivery.

Is there an exam for postman recruitment?

Yes. India Post conducts a computer-based test covering General Knowledge, Mathematics, English and the regional language. Some posts like Gramin Dak Sevak (GDS) are selected on the basis of Class 10 marks without a written exam.

How do I apply for a postman job in 2026?

Apply online through the official India Post recruitment portal at indiapostgdsonline.gov.in (for GDS) or the SSC / circle notification for Postman/Mail Guard posts. Watch for circle-wise notifications, fill the form, pay the fee, and upload documents before the deadline.

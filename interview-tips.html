Interview Tips

Ace your next interview with these practical tips

💡 Use HireTrack's AI Interview Prep on your Profile page to practise role-specific questions with instant feedback.

Before the Interview

  • Research the company — Know their products, recent news, leadership, and culture. Check LinkedIn and their website 30 minutes before.
  • Study the job description — Map your experience to every key requirement. Prepare a specific example for each skill mentioned.
  • Prepare your story — Have a crisp 90-second answer for "Tell me about yourself" that covers your background, key achievement, and why you're here.
  • Prepare questions to ask — Never say "No, I have no questions." Ask about team structure, success metrics, or growth opportunities.

During the Interview

  • Use the STAR method — For behavioural questions: Situation, Task, Action, Result. Keep it concise and specific.
  • Quantify your impact — "I improved report turnaround time by 40%" beats "I made reporting faster."
  • Pause before answering — It's okay to take 3–5 seconds to think. Thoughtful answers beat rushed ones.
  • Ask for clarification — If a question is unclear, ask the interviewer to rephrase rather than guessing.
  • Match their energy — Be warm, confident, and professional. A genuine smile goes further than you think.

For Technical Interviews

  • Think out loud — interviewers want to see your reasoning process, not just the answer.
  • Start with a brute-force approach, then optimise. Show structured thinking.
  • For SQL/Excel rounds: practice common scenarios — aggregations, joins, pivot tables, VLOOKUP.
  • For data analysis rounds: always clarify assumptions before diving into numbers.

Salary Negotiation

  • Never give a number first. Ask "What is the budgeted range for this role?"
  • Research market rates on LinkedIn Salary, Glassdoor, and AmbitionBox before the discussion.
  • Negotiate on total compensation — base, bonus, ESOPs, joining bonus, and notice period buyout.
  • Always take 24–48 hours before accepting. It's expected and respected.

After the Interview

  • Send a thank-you email within 24 hours — brief, genuine, and reiterating your interest.
  • Follow up after the committed timeline if you haven't heard back. One polite follow-up is professional, not pushy.
  • If rejected, ask for feedback — most companies will share it and it helps you improve.
